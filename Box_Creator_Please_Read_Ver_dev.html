Please Read Before Use

Box Size are Internal Usable Dimensions

  • Important: All Box Dimensions entered in this Gadget represent the Internal Usable Size of the Box.
  • For example, a Box entered as 12" X 12" X 12" will have approximately 12" of Internal Width, Depth, and height.
  • The Finished Outside Dimensions will be larger depending on Material Thickness and the Selected Lid and Bottom Configuration.
  • Side and End Panel Heights are Automatically Adjusted where Required to Maintain the Specified Internal Dimensions.

More Important Information:

  • At Job Setup, use the Lower Left-Hand Corner as the XY Datum Location.
  • This Gadget Creates Two Sets of Vectors for Each Box Part on Two Separate Layers (Box and CutOut). The First (Box Layer) Vector Shows the Box Part Shape. The Second (CutOut Layer) Vector Shows & is the Actual Toolpath Set to ON the Vector.
  • The Cutout Toolpath Vector was Retained for Three Reasons: Out of Respect for the Original Gadget Author and The Cutout Toolpath Vector contains Proven Dogbones that provide Proper Interference-Fit Assembly.
  • Even though Dogbones are for Dovetails. Consistency remains to be Key throughout the Gadget.
  • Please use Caution on the Fluting Toolpath Plunge Rate Feed Speed.
  • Flat Lid Panel does not Automatically create a Recess Pocket. Recess Pocket is Now USER OPTIONAL!
  • Use Add Recess Pocket only when a Recessed Flat Lid or Flat Bottom Panels are Desired.
  • When the No Toolpath Checkbox is Selected, the Gadget creates Vectors Only.

CAUTION: CLOSE PART PLACEMENT

  • When Box Parts are placed Close Together, Small Pieces of Waste Material may be created between Cutout Vectors.
  • If the Cutout Toolpath is run without holding tabs, these Small Pieces can become Loose and may be Struck or Thrown by the Router Bit.
  • Recommendations:
  • Use Holding Tabs whenever possible on Cutout Toolpaths.
  • Increase the Part Gap when Needed.
  • Review the 2D/3D Previews before Machining.
  • Remove or Secure Small Waste Pieces when Safe to Do So.

Special Geometry Considerations (Dovetails Joint Type)

Problem Example



Solution Example

  • In Rare Cases, certain Combinations of Box Dimensions and Joint Widths may create a very Small Remaining Feature that is Smaller than the Selected Cutout Tool Diameter.
  • Message Box Message --"The Selected Tool will not fit between the Joint Width. Increase Joint Width."
  • Problem:
  • Box Size: 8" X 8" X 8"
  • Joint Width: 1.0"
  • Remaining Feature: approximately 0.057"
  • Similar Small-Feature Conditions may occur with other Box Dimensions and Joint-Width Combinations.
  • Since the Cutter cannot Physically Fit into this Area, a Small Amount of Material may Remain after Machining.
  • Solution:
  • Increase the Joint Width Slightly. Increasing by 1/8" Corrected this Example during Testing.
  • Adjust the Box Dimensions.
  • Use a Smaller Cutter where Appropriate.
  • Testing Status: Actual Machining Tests were Performed on Mating Dovetail Joints.
  • Full Box Assembly Testing is Ongoing.
  • Always Review the 2D and 3D Previews before Machining.
